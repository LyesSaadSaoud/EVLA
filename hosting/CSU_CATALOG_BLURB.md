# CSU Catalog Blurb

## Short Description

EVLA is a physics-grounded Energy Vision-Language-Action benchmark for residential energy management. It supports research on multimodal, intent-conditioned energy decision learning by combining energy-field visual representations, explicit physical state metadata, natural-language objectives, and 16-step battery-action trajectories.

## Longer Description

The EVLA benchmark contains 439,203 retained base operating states expanded across three hidden visual-energy regimes and five language objectives, yielding 6,588,045 multimodal samples. The dataset is designed for controlled counterfactual evaluation: the same physical state can be evaluated under different hidden visual-energy regimes and semantic energy objectives such as energy saving, comfort, balanced operation, grid support, and eco operation.

EVLA is intended for reproducible AI research in residential energy management. It is not a certified deployment-ready home-energy controller.
